GES App Development

UI/UX Design

Company Overview

GES team of over 3,000 designers, account managers, drivers, advisors and electricians use their skills to deliver the perfect combination of art and science to help customers and clients to plan, design and build their brands at all kinds of exhibitions around the world.

Background

My professor in Newhouse was assigned to design the mobile app for GES, based on their existing web app Expresso, to help users to plan, design and clean up show stands in pre-show, in-show, and after-show phases. The app will give assistance in ordering and managing users' purchases, and a great deal of information. As an intern, I assisted him in research and design process.

My Role

  • Conducted research of the existing web app Expresso
  • Made assessment of the functions -- maintain primary ones and eliminate secondary functions -- of the mobile app based on research results
  • Assisted in designing drafts of interaction map and mockups
  • Design mockups using Balsamiq, Illustrator and Photoshop

The Difficulties

Show planning is a very complicated process. Before the show, customers need assistance in ordering and managing their purchases, and a great deal of information to feel reassured that they will have a productive experience. When they are in show, they need quick and easy-to-reach support if any emergency happens. After the show, customers need fast and orderly cleaning up and logistic service.

The existing web app Expresso, has functions to provide complete service along the process. However, if all these functions are moved to the mobile app, it will be too complicated for on-the-go users to use, and may very likely deteriorate user experience. Thus, the mobile app should have all necessary primary functions and, at the same time, easy to use.
Therefore, the first task was to evaluate all functions of Expresso. And then chose primary functions, and eliminate secondary functions.



The Initial Draft

After conducting research and case studies, I assisted professor in choosing the necessary functions for pre-show planning phase, and made the first draft of customer journey map.

Learn by Doing

It was the first time for me to make a customer journey map. I found it a very important and necessary step in app development. Just list all steps and functions is not enough, we may easily oversee some process which seems not that important. By imaging yourself as a customer, and going through every process step by step, it's much easier to organize thoughts, and to check if everything is included.

The Mockup

By turning the ideas into mockups, we were able to better convey the design to clients. We then modified the design according to their feedbacks. through this back-and-forth process, the utility, usability and user experience of the app has been improved.
