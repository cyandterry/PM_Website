Business Design for Syracuse New Times

User Research | UX Design

BACKGROUND

Syracuse New Times, a lccal newspaper, came to us and assigned us with two problems:

  • Reader engagement: In what ways can we get our readers more engaged with our brand for the benefit of our advertisers?
  • Brand extension: Are there any viable (profitable) brand extension opportunities for the Syracuse New Times in the market place? If so, what are they?

MY ROLE & WHAT I HAVE DONE

User researcher, designer

  • Competitor analysis
  • Analyze the Publication Audit Report of SNT in terms of Circulation, Distribution, Net Press Average, and supplemental reader studies
  • In-depth Interviews with 2 readers
  • Designed final presentation ppt

RESULTS & DELIVERABLES

All of our advices have been adopted by Syracuse New Times. With our help, their both online and offline performance and user experience have improved a lot.
Our project has been ranked 1st in class.

Market Research

After comprehensive market research, which includes Syracuse New Times Publication Audit Report for 3 consecutive years, competitor analysis, demographic studies, and supplemental reader studies, we got some statistics about SNT's current performance:

  • 34,683 print copies of Circulation in 2012, YTY growth rate is 23.1%
  • Spread over 1100 various local spots for free in Central New York area
  • Advertising accounts for 55% of the content
we found some unique features of Syracuse New Times that can be seen as great opportunities to succeed:
  • Educated and affluent people in their 21 to 54 accounts for the main readership
    • 63% of the readers have an annual income over $50,000
  • 76% of them are college graduates
  • The events information is unique and very useful for readers


Discover Problems

We used AIDA concept (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action) to examine every stage of user's interaction with Syracuse New Times. We found some problems as follow:

  • Awareness -- social: Acting social, not real social
  • Awareness -- search: The brand name dilutes the word-spaces. Keywords appear the same on each page, resulting a low page quality score for pages other than homepage.
  • Interest – Website and Mobile: Structurally, not user friendly; Identical content on every platform; No Android platform coverage

So, we discovered a more effective approach:
From one-size-fits-all content approach to personalized content to the individual customer, what they need, and when and where they require.


Interviews, Personas and Scenarios

We conducted 10 interviews with SNT readers or potential readers chosen on the basis of our initial reader studies. Altough they represented different ages, jobs, needs and lifestyle, they all might need some kind of local information about CNY area. We tried to answer two questions out of these interviews:

  • In what ways can we get the readers more engaged with SNT brand?
  • Are there any viable (profitable) brand extension opportunities?
    • After the interviews, we then designed personas and scenarios based on the information we have got. I summarized the findings, and created 5 personas and scenarios.

Strategic Recommendations


Social + Visual

Move the social battlefield to Visual-oriented sites like Youtube and Instagram.
From social media’s annual growth chart compared from Y2012 to Y2011, the Top 4 winners are “Instagram, Tumblr, Google+ and Youtube”. It indicates that readers prefer visual content more over on text-savvy lines on Social.

Customized Content

The discovery of preferences of different reader groups results in the ability to provide personalized content.
Require that editors add persona tags to every article posted; Add a filter function on the website that enables content sorting by tags.

Let Data Talk!

  • Data-oriented SNT site: Embed Google Analytics code to the whole site
  • Customer Data Collection: Collect Customer Data at each touch point, both online and offline
  • Rewarding System: Two-way offline online interaction
