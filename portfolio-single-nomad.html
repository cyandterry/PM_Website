Nomad Mobile Magazine

UI/UX Design, Advertising, Editorial Design


Background

Newhouse was going to launch a mobile magazine, and we were asked to develop a complete and doable plan for them, which includes content strategy, business plan and magazine prototype.

What I Have Done

  • Interviewed 5 people
  • Studied sucessful examples
  • Conducted competitor analysis
  • Design wireframes and prototype
  • Design pitch ppt

My Role

  • A member of a team of 3
  • User Researcher
  • UX/UI Designer

Brainstorm

We brainstormed for 3 rounds. In the first round, we write down whatever came to our minds. After 2nd discussion, we screened the ideas pool to only 9 ideas, they are as follow:

  • New Yorker, Rent, Daily Magazine, 21-25, roommate，interior decoration，three questions, interactive map, video, photo
  • Bowling 35-45, social, entertaining, family
  • Headphones
  • Lego, colorful, entertaining, creative
  • Lighter
  • Pets
  • Archery
  • Donut/sandwich/pretzels
  • five-a-side soccer/football

Finally we chose only one idea left, Nomad:
A magazine which updates on daily basis. Each issue will cover one story via multimedia platforms about a person who newly arrived in the New York City to start own career. And we gave it a romantic name, Nomad.

Interviews and Persona

We interviewed 10 students in Newhouse. All of them are either senior students or graduate students who are going to graduate in 4 months' time, and are actively looking for jobs in New York City.

We analyzed the interviews, and summarized the persona as follow:
21-25 years old, with bachelor degree or above, who is looking for a roommate in New York City due to financial restraint. He is eager to seek a fitted roommate but not through boring online searching. He prefers a unique experience. He knows the life would be not easy in New York City, but he hopes he will enjoy there. He likes stories, and people who have different experiences. He also loves to share his story. No doubt, he is social media savvy person. Sharing and interactive are always enjoyable for him.

Conception & Content Strategy

Simple

  • Simple concept: one person, one story, one quote, one video
  • Simple content: A feature story about a young person who comes to New York City to pursue his/her own career goal and happiness
  • A 15-second video that let the the subject tell the fun facts about him/herself
  • A key quote comes from the story that can best represent the subject

Entertaining

  • Entertaining concept: People will tell the fun facts about themselves in a short video. The reporting team will make sure that even a serious person can make you laugh through the video, in its own ways.
  • Entertaining advertisements: Nomad will allow users to interact with the advertisements. They could win a coupon after playing the small games on the advertising page.

Humane

There are a thousand New York Cities in a thousand people’s eyes. Nomad will tell you the story about those recently graduated student who just started their lives in the city, which could be tough, cruel, sweet, and touching, but all in a very intimate and humanized way. The magazine will uncover the unique stories under the seemingly similar lives.

Digital Strategy & Business Plan

Search Engine Optimization

We already know that Nomad is a spopular keyword. We need to use some smart SEO tricks to win this battlefield. Instead of wasting our advertising budget on the popular word 'nomad', we will bet on related keywords. In terms of SEO campaign, we will optimize both short-tail keywords like 'New Yorker', 'New York housing', 'life in New York City'. And long-tail keywords like 'finding roommate in New York city', 'finding apartment in New York city', 'share apartment'.

Twitter

By using Hootsuite or similar services, an automate tweet about new issue with short links and a image will be posted on time. We will keep our consistent young and energetic tone. We may also tweet some on-going activities in New York City to create interactions with readers. Different hashtags are going to be used to hold a weekly twitter chat, for example, #LifeInNYC. We tend to build up a active community for New York City renters and newcomers.

Facebook

Our Facebook account will focus more on videos. Since we will record a short and funny video of our interviewees. We have plenty of materials to show everyday. Besides our original videos, we will also share other viral and interesting videos on internet. Also, we will create several related or unrelated fan page to help promote our content, like 'What to Buy in NYC Today?', to solely drive more traffic to our content.

Pinterest/Instagram

Similar to Facebook shares, we will bombard visual elements in these two social media. However, this strategy aims to female readers and potential readers. In terms of Pinterest, an official account will collect relevant pictures about NYC renters community on every aspect, like restaurants, room decorations, 10 minute dinner recipe, etc.. With regard to Instagram, several hashtag-driven events will be launched.

Email

You may think email newsletter is out of date. However, it’s essential of digital marketing. We will try to negotiate with colleges in NYC and around NYC to obtain email-list for sending newsletters. Besides, the person who downloads our magazine will be softly opt-ined our email list. We’ll make sure keep email newsletter relevant to Nomad’s content and keep in a reasonable frequency.

Wikipedia

This is kind of innovative way to build an entry for Nomad. We will add new definition to the word 'nomad' in Wikipedia, and also create the word 'Nomad Magazine'. Links will be surely included. It should be noticed that SEO would be a critical part of this tactic. When people search Nomad, the first entry should be our magazine.
