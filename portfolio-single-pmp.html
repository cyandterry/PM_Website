PMP Website Redesign

UX Design | Web Design | SEO | Content Management

BACKGROUND

PMP is a publishing company who is dedicated to bringing the best marketing books to readers. What's unique about PMP is that their books are designed with many charts, tables and margin notes, so readers can extract a large amount of information in a short period of time. However, because of confusing website structure, poor design and low SEO effort, PMP website is buried in the sea of marketing information.

Task & Goal

Our task was to conduct user research, competitor analysis, content analysis, and evaluate website structure to discover existing problems, And then redesign the website to increase its user experience, and raise searchability and page ranking. We want PMP to be recognized as an authoritative publishing company of marketing books after the website redesign.

My Role

Designer & Content Analyst
  • Conducted user research and competitor analysis
  • Reorganized website content and information architecture to improve its search engine results and user experience
  • Created wireframes and visual mockups
  • Edited and designed the final PMP Website Optimization report


The Customer Survey

We sent out 36 surveys to existing and potential PMP customers, and conducted 5 in-depth interviews. We got some important findings that would later guide the website redesign:

1. Female consists of the majority of the readers
This imbalance represents a possible growth area for PMP. It can focus more on the books that will appeal to the male market segment.

2. “Market Research” and “Teaching Others” are the primary interest areas
“Market Research” is a well-defined category that contains books of quantitative and qualitative research methods. We believe that “Teaching Others” indicates a need for textbooks. Small business owners, educators and students make up half of the customer base; they need textbooks for teaching purpose.

3. Need for a fresh-look website interface design
Customers are not satisfied with the website design, though PMP got “average” satis- faction level in the multiple-choice section of the survey. People thought of website as “old-school” and needs to “freshen up its look”.

Google Analytics Findings

We installed Google Analytics on PMP site and started to track online data for 1 month. Although the results are not gained through a long period time, we could still find some valuable suggestions:

1. The potential market of Niche Marketing
From the “visitors flow” chart, we discovered that the webpage for the book “85% Niche” acquires the most traffic except the homepage. we believe that this reveals a big market for books about niche marketing.

2. People are searching for titles instead of authors
Keywords from Google Analytics show that people are searching book tittles yet few people search author names. This graph is evidence that a successful search-based acquisition strategy should be built on titles as keywords.

Keywords Strategy

To better understand PMP’s performance on search engine, we conducted Google search for Black Still Matters In Marketing and The Art of Strategic Interviewing, and developed keyword strategy based on our findings.

PMP outperformed Amazon in books that don’t contain bigger category information such as “Marketing”.
Based on this finding, we assume that reinforcing the “Category Layer” on each page of the PMP website will improve the search result in a large scale. Thus keywords containing information of “Author”, “Books”, and “Category” should be built in html code and appear frequently on each page.



Recategorization

Current situation:
The current categories module is polluting the site’s wordspace
The repetition of unrelated terms (structured as html links) on all the site’s pages reduces the content quality score for individual pages, and this has a detrimental effect on search rank. When search engines index a page, they analyze all the words in relative context to get a sense of ‘what the page is about’. This lowers the content quality score for all the book pages.

Objective Methods:
Probabilistic search and Google Trends
Researching frequently searched marketing related keywords in Google, we would incorporate those words into book page to drive traffic which is interested in the search terms.

Finally, we have created five new topics that we believe will better suit the Paramount website: Niche Marketing, Multicultural Marketing, Marketing Research, Marketing Functions and Marketing Outliers. Most of these categories then have more descriptive sectors beneath the landing pages that allow books to be classified more specifically.
